## Notes

### VC Investment Process
1. Pitch to investors - 2w-2y
2. Invectors decide to invest - 2w-4m
3. Term sheet negotiation 2-4 weeks
4. Financing Documentation (Lawyer Time) - 4-12w
    - Lawyer Time
    - 4-12w
    - Draft legal docs: Purchase Agreement, Articles of Incorporation, Voting Agreements, etc.
    - Prepare closing deliverables: legal certs, government filings, etc.
5. Sign and close (Big Cash Money)
6. Additional closings: milestones, tranches, etc.

## Term Sheet Definitions

Term Sheet
- Guts of the business deal
- NOT a legal document
- short 5-8 pages
- VC offers it's template

Common Stock
- What founders and employees get
- Voting rights only (usually)
- Very cheap, especially at round A
- Options and restricted stock

Preferred Stock
- What VCs get
- "Preferred" because it's got better rights and protections than common stock
- Costs more than common stock
- What defines "preferences" is a key focus of negotiation

Valuation
- Pre-money: value before financing
- Post-money: pre-money + financing
- VC stake as a percentage of post-money
    - 4 on 6 =
    - $6M pre-money with $4M round =
    - $10 M post-money total; VC owns 40% of company
    - VC gets round / post-money

## Control, Ownership, & Economic Power
5 Key Terms to Negotiate:
- Board of Directors
- Vesting
- Option Pool
- Preferred Stock Perks: Participation
- Preferred Stock Perks: Anti-Dilution

### Board of Directors
- Governing group of company
- Approves major strategic decisions
- Does not have an operating role
- Shareholders elect, often by class vote
- Pre-money -- usually consists of employees only
- Post-money -- a mix of VCs, employees, outsiders
